First, Let's Define It

"A brand is what people say about you when you're not in the room."

— Jeff Bezos, Amazon founder

What is a Brand?

A brand is NOT:

A logo
Colors and fonts
A website
A tagline

→

A brand IS:

A feeling people have
Your reputation
Trust built over time
A promise you keep

Think of it This Way

A brand lives in people's minds

You
→
Your Actions
→
Their Feeling

Every interaction shapes how people feel about you. That feeling IS your brand.

Why This Matters

Strong brands charge more and keep clients longer.

Real Numbers

Apple charges 2-3x more than competitors. Four Seasons charges $800/night. Mayo Clinic has year-long waitlists. That's what brand does.

01

Step 1: Purpose

What is Purpose?

Purpose is the reason your brand exists beyond profit. It's the change you want to make in the world. It's what gets you out of bed.

The Key Question to Ask Yourself

"Why does this matter to the world?"

01

Step 1: Purpose — Example

Nike

"Just Do It"

WHAT THEY SELL

Shoes and sportswear

THEIR PURPOSE

"To bring inspiration and innovation to every athlete in the world"

Why This Works

They don't sell shoes. They sell the belief that everyone is an athlete. That's why "Just Do It" resonates—it's about human potential, not footwear.

01

Step 1: Purpose — Example

Patagonia

"Don't Buy This Jacket"

WHAT THEY SELL

Outdoor clothing

THEIR PURPOSE

"We're in business to save our home planet"

Why This Works

They ran an ad saying "Don't Buy This Jacket." Crazy? No—it proves they care about the planet more than sales. Customers trust them completely.

Position

How are you different from everyone else?

02

Step 2: Position

What is Positioning?

Positioning is the unique space you occupy in people's minds. It's not about being better—it's about being different in a way that matters.

The Key Question to Ask Yourself

"Why should someone choose me over everyone else?"

02

Step 2: Position — Example

Mayo Clinic

"The needs of the patient come first"

Their Position

"Where you go when it really matters."

They're not the cheapest. They're not the most convenient. But when it's serious, people fly across the world to go there. That's positioning.

02

Step 2: Position — Example

Apple

"Think Different"

Their Position

"Premium technology for creative people who want things to just work."

They never compete on specs or price. They compete on experience and identity. Owning Apple says something about who you are.

Personality

If your brand were a person, who would they be?

03

Step 3: Personality

What is Brand Personality?

Personality is the human traits of your brand. It determines how you talk, how you look, and how people feel when they interact with you.

The Key Question to Ask Yourself

"If my brand walked into a room, what would people think?"

03

Step 3: Personality — Example

Four Seasons

"The Golden Rule"

Their Personality

Warm. Attentive. Sophisticated. Anticipates your needs before you ask. Like a gracious host who remembers everything about you.

Their staff remember your name, your coffee order, your preferences. That's not a policy—that's personality in action.

03

Step 3: Personality — Example

Ritz-Carlton

"Ladies and Gentlemen serving Ladies and Gentlemen"

Their Personality

Formal. Dignified. Empowering. Every employee can spend $2,000 to solve a guest problem—no approval needed.

This creates legendary service stories that people share. The personality becomes the marketing.

Step 4: Visual Identity

What is Visual Identity?

Visual identity is colors, fonts, logo, and imagery. It must reflect your purpose, position, and personality. It's the last step—not the first.

The Key Question to Ask Yourself

"Does this look like who we are?"

04

Step 4: Visual Identity

Why is this step last?

Visual Identity
Personality
Positioning
Purpose

Most people start with visuals and work down. That's backwards. Start with purpose, and the visuals become obvious.
