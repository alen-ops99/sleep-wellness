Part 01

What is a Brand, Really?

"A brand is not what you say it is. It's what they say it is."

— Marty Neumeier, The Brand Gap

A Brand is NOT

  • Just a logo
  • Just colors and fonts
  • Just a tagline
  • Just a website
  • Something you can fully control

A Brand IS

  • A person's gut feeling about you
  • The sum of all experiences with you
  • A promise you make and keep
  • Trust built over time
  • Your reputation when you're not in the room

The Distinction

Logo vs. Identity vs. Brand

1

Logo

A symbol that identifies your company. The simplest, most recognizable element.

Example: The Nike Swoosh

2

Visual Identity

The complete visual system: logo, colors, typography, imagery, design patterns.

Example: Nike's black/white, bold type, athlete photography

3

Brand

Everything people think and feel about you. Built through every interaction.

Example: Nike = athletic excellence, pushing limits, "Just Do It" mindset

Personality Traits

  • Scientific but accessible
  • Sophisticated but not cold
  • Confident but not arrogant

Brand Character

The Trusted Expert

Element 04

Values: Your Non-Negotiables

Values aren't aspirational posters on a wall. They're the principles that guide difficult decisions.

Evidence-Based

Every recommendation backed by peer-reviewed research. No trends, no fads, no pseudoscience. If it's not proven, we don't recommend it.

In practice: You cite sources. You say "I don't know" when you don't. You update recommendations when new research emerges.

Excellence

We work with clients who demand the best because we demand it of ourselves. Premium service requires premium commitment from both sides.

In practice: You don't discount. You don't compromise on quality. You say no to clients who aren't serious.

Transformation

We're not here to make small improvements. We're here to fundamentally change how people and organizations approach rest and recovery.

In practice: You measure outcomes. You track before/after. You only claim results you can demonstrate.

Element 05

Voice: How You Speak

Voice Attributes

Authoritative

Speak from expertise. Use declarative statements. Don't hedge unnecessarily.

Not: "Sleep might help with recovery"
But: "Sleep is when 70% of growth hormone is released"

Warm

Professional doesn't mean cold. Connect personally. Show you care about outcomes.

Not: "Clients will receive protocols"
But: "We design protocols around your life"

Clear

Translate complex science into accessible insights. Never use jargon to impress.

Not: "Circadian rhythm dysregulation"
But: "When your internal clock is out of sync"

Tone Spectrum

More formal → Website, proposals, publications

Balanced → Consultations, presentations, newsletters

More casual → Social media, personal emails, interviews

Words We Use

Optimize, transform, precision, evidence-based, performance, restorative, strategic, elite

Words We Avoid

Hack, trick, secret, revolutionary, miracle, guaranteed, disrupting

Element 06

Visual Identity: The Look

Color Psychology

Deep Navy
Trust, expertise, depth

Gold
Premium, excellence, warmth

Cream
Calm, clean, sophisticated

Typography

Cormorant Garamond

Elegant serif for headlines. Conveys sophistication, tradition, authority.

Montserrat

Clean sans-serif for body. Modern, readable, professional.

Visual Principles

  • Generous white space — Luxury brands breathe. Don't crowd.
  • High contrast — Navy on cream, gold accents. Clear hierarchy.
  • Elegant simplicity — Refined, not busy. Quality over quantity.
  • Professional photography — No stock photos of people fake-sleeping.
  • Subtle textures — Hints of warmth, not sterile clinical white.

Imagery Style

Do: Elegant bedroom environments, professional headshots, abstract representations of rest, architectural details of luxury hotels, athletes in peak condition

Don't: Cheesy stock photos, clip art, generic business imagery, cartoons, busy collages

Experience: Every Touchpoint

Your brand is built or destroyed at every interaction. Map every touchpoint and make each one intentional.

Awareness

  • Website visit
  • Social media post
  • Media feature
  • Word of mouth
  • Speaking event

Consideration

Evaluation

  • Discovery call
  • Proposal/deck
  • Email exchanges
  • References check
  • Pricing discussion

Purchase

Commitment

  • Contract signing
  • Onboarding process
  • First session
  • Welcome materials
  • Payment experience

Loyalty

Relationship

  • Ongoing service
  • Check-ins
  • Results reporting
  • Referral program
  • Continued value

The Experience Question

At each touchpoint, ask: "Does this feel like a Harvard-trained sleep physician who works with elite clients?" If not, fix it. Everything communicates—your email signature, your Zoom background, your invoice design.

Part 04 — Real Examples

Apple: The Masterclass

Apple Technology / Consumer Electronics
Purpose

Challenge the status quo. Think different. Make technology human.

Positioning

Premium technology that "just works" for creative, discerning people.

Personality

Innovative, sleek, confident, minimalist, premium.

Values

Simplicity, innovation, premium quality, user experience, privacy.

Why Apple Works

  • Consistency: Every touchpoint feels "Apple"—stores, packaging, software, ads
  • Clarity: You know exactly what Apple stands for
  • Premium positioning: Never competes on price, always on value
  • Emotional connection: "Think Different" appeals to identity, not specs

Lesson for Sleep Wellness

  • Every touchpoint should feel "Dr. Alen"—sophisticated, scientific, warm
  • Never compete on price with generic sleep coaches
  • Appeal to identity: "For those who demand excellence"
  • Packaging matters: proposals, reports, materials should all feel premium

Real Example

Four Seasons: Luxury Service

Four Seasons Luxury Hospitality
Purpose

To perfect the travel experience through innovation and service.

Positioning

The world's leading luxury hospitality brand. Uncompromising quality.

Personality

Warm, attentive, sophisticated, understated, anticipatory.

Values

Golden Rule (treat others as you wish to be treated), excellence, innovation.

For Sleep Wellness: Remember client details. Follow up unprompted. Anticipate questions before they ask. This is how you justify premium pricing.

Part 05

The Brand Building Framework

A practical 5-step process to build your brand systematically.

1
Discover

Audit current state. Research competitors. Interview clients. Understand perceptions.

2
Define

Purpose, positioning, personality, values. The strategic foundation.

3
Design

Visual identity, voice guidelines, messaging framework. The system.

4
Deploy

Website, materials, templates, touchpoints. Consistent execution.

5
Develop

Ongoing refinement. Gather feedback. Evolve while staying consistent.
